Say Hello To Storm-DFT

A probabilistic risk assessment tool

Proper risk analysis is essential to keep our power plants, trains and self-driving cars safe and operational. Fault trees are a key technique here, used on a daily basis by millions of engineers around the world. Their application includes aerospace, nuclear power, car, and process engineering industries. Various fault tree extensions exist that increase expressiveness while yielding succinct and comprehensible models. Their analysis is however a main bottleneck: techniques do not scale and require substantial manual effort.

The Formal Methods and Tools Group at the University of Twente and the MOVES Group at RWTH Aachen University, has developed several fully automated fault-tree analysis techniques that has won several academic tool competitions, and is at the frontier of state-of-the art. It goes beyond the capabilities of existing commercial tools for fault tree analysis in offering more flexible modelling and analyses: easier handling of different parameter settings, more metrics, richer and larger fault trees, and offers assessments with different levels of precision. Applications with industrial partners in railway systems, smart buildings, car manufacturing, aerospace systems, and energy networks have demonstrated the maturity and power of our software tool.
